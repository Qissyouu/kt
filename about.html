ABOUT KOTA TINGGI

Kota Tinggi, a district in Johor, Malaysia

is home to a diverse community of 200,000 people.

Including farmers, fishermen, industrial workers,

and government employees.



The district features traditional villages,

Felda settlements,and new KEJORA townships

with many residents also working in nearby industrial zones.



Efforts to modernize agriculture aim to attract

younger generations. Rich in historical significance as

the birthplace of the Johor Malay Sultanate,



Kota Tinggi boasts ancient tombs, cities, and tourist

attractions like the Kota Tinggi waterfalls and Desaru beaches.



The local cuisine, especially 'belangkas' (horseshoe crab),

is a beloved delicacy among residents and Johor's royalty.
