Rich and deeply intertwined

District's name, Kota Tinggi, which means "High Fort"

The history of Kota Tinggi is rich and deeply intertwined with the cultural and political evolution of Johor and the broader Malay world. The district's name, Kota Tinggi, which means "High Fort" in Malay, was given by Sultan Mahmud because of the high ground on which it was built, compared to the river level.

Kota Tinggi's significance

Continuation of the Malacca Sultanate after the Portuguese captured Malacca in 1511

Kota Tinggi's significance dates back to the period of the Johor Malay Sultanate, which was a continuation of the Malacca Sultanate after the Portuguese captured Malacca in 1511. Sultan Alauddin Riayat Shah II established the Johor Sultanate in 1528, making it one of the most powerful empires in the Malay Peninsula. Kota Tinggi, strategically located at the estuary of Sungai Johor, became an important center for administration and trade.

The district is historically significant

Influenced by various civilizations, including those of Malacca, Johor-Riau, Singapore, and Linggi.

The district is historically significant as it was influenced by various civilizations, including those of Malacca, Johor-Riau, Singapore, and Linggi. The Sungai Johor (Johor River) played a vital role in the prosperity of ancient Johor, serving as a major trade route. Along its banks, numerous historical sites and remains from the Johor Sultanate era can still be found, such as Makam-Makam Lama (ancient tombs) and Kota-Kota Lama (ancient cities), which stand as testaments to the region's glorious past.

Notable historical events

Assassination of Sultan Mahmud Shah II in 1699

One of the notable historical events associated with Kota Tinggi is the assassination of Sultan Mahmud Shah II in 1699. This event marked the end of the direct line of the Malacca Sultanate, leading to a period of political instability and succession disputes within the Johor-Riau Empire.

Kota Tinggi continued to develop as a vital part of Johor's administration and economy

Stablishment of new towns and settlements

In the later centuries, Kota Tinggi continued to develop as a vital part of Johor's administration and economy. The establishment of new towns and settlements by Lembaga Kemajuan Johor Tenggara (KEJORA) further accelerated its growth. Bandar Kota Tinggi emerged as the main administrative center, while other towns like Pekan Sungai Rengit, Pekan Teluk Sengat, and Pekan Sedili Besar played significant roles in the district's socio-economic landscape.

Involved in various agricultural developments

Promoting the cultivation of oil palm and rubber

The district was also involved in various agricultural developments, particularly with the establishment of Felda settlements aimed at promoting the cultivation of oil palm and rubber. These initiatives helped transform the economic activities of the local population, ensuring sustainable livelihoods and contributing to the region's overall prosperity.

Kota Tinggi's historical and cultural heritage

Prominent tourist destination

Kota Tinggi's historical and cultural heritage, combined with its natural beauty, makes it a prominent tourist destination. The district is renowned for its scenic spots like the Kota Tinggi waterfalls, Panti Recreational Forest, and picturesque beaches such as Desaru and Tanjung Balau. These attractions, along with its rich historical narrative, draw visitors from within Malaysia and neighboring countries like Singapore and Indonesia.
